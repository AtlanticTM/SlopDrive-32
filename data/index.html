⚡ Motor Driver (TMC2160)

Tune live: changes apply to the driver immediately when you press Apply Live, so you can feel the difference without saving. Save stores them to flash so they persist after reboot.
2000 mA
More current = more torque and holding force, but more heat in the motor and driver. Raise if the motor stalls/skips under load; lower if it runs hot. 1500-2500mA is typical for a NEMA17/23 stroker.
50 %
Percentage of run current used to hold position when stopped. Lower = cooler and quieter at rest, but weaker holding force. 30-50% is usually fine.
SpreadCycle gives the strongest, most consistent torque - best for a machine that pushes against load. StealthChop is whisper-quiet but can lose torque at higher speeds. If you want quiet at low speed AND torque at high speed, pick StealthChop and set the threshold below.
0
Only relevant in StealthChop mode. The driver runs StealthChop below this speed and automatically switches to SpreadCycle above it (for torque). 0 = never switch (stay in the chosen mode always). Higher number = switches at a lower speed. Tune by ear.
4
Sets the chopper's off-time. Must be 1-15 for the driver to run (0 disables it). Affects audible tone and low-speed smoothness. 3-5 is a safe range; leave at 4 unless chasing a specific noise.
